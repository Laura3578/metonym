Intellectual Property & Patent Notice.

What is openly published, what is patent-pending, what is claimed as a Metonym trademark, and how to cite or request permissions.

Effective date: 2026-05-16 · Last updated: 2026-05-16

1. Mechanical Severity Score — patent pending (no license granted by site visit)

The Mechanical Severity Score (MSS) is a patent-pending structured scoring procedure that operationalizes the clinical judgment described in the SDM into a reproducible measurement. MSS is the answer to the question: how do we measure it the same way every time?

Patent status

  • US Provisional Patent Application No. 64/059,837, filed 2026-05-07
  • Inventor: Laura L. Walsh, PsyD
  • Assignee: Metonym LLC
  • Status: Patent pending. No patent has issued. A non-provisional application is required by 2027-05-07.

No license granted

Nothing on this Site, in the SDM preprint, in any informal communication with Metonym (including a scoping call), or in any deliverable received as part of a Metonym engagement grants you a license to:

  • Operate the MSS scoring procedure on your own or any third party's behalf
  • Use the MSS scoring procedure, evaluation pipeline, rubric anchors, or scenario library in any commercial product or service
  • Build, train, or operate any AI/ML system using the MSS scoring procedure as input or training signal

Operational use of the MSS by third parties is reserved to Metonym engagements. Commercial use of the MSS by any third party requires a separate written license from Metonym LLC.

What is not reserved

The SDM clinical framework (Section 2 below) is openly published under CC-BY-NC 4.0 and is not affected by the patent claim. Discussion, citation, teaching, and non-commercial academic use of the SDM remains freely permitted.

2. Salient Distress Model — openly published (CC-BY-NC 4.0)

The Salient Distress Model (SDM) is Dr. Laura L. Walsh's clinical-theoretical framework defining the categories of psychological signal a competent evaluator should look for in conversations involving distress. SDM is the answer to the question: what should an evaluator notice?

Publication

The SDM is formally documented in the Metonym methodology preprint, openly archived on Zenodo:

Walsh, L. L. (2026). [Methodology preprint title]. Zenodo.
https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20147000

The preprint is not yet peer-reviewed; journal submission is in progress. Readers should treat empirical findings accordingly.

License — Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial 4.0 International (CC-BY-NC 4.0)

The SDM framework and the preprint text are released under CC-BY-NC 4.0. In plain English:

  • You may cite the SDM in your own research, teaching, training, and other non-commercial work.
  • You may share, adapt, and build upon the SDM framework for non-commercial purposes.
  • You must attribute the work to Walsh, L. L. (2026) and link to the DOI.
  • You may not use the SDM for commercial purposes without separate written permission from Metonym LLC.

"Commercial purposes" includes selling products or services that incorporate the SDM, offering paid evaluation services using the SDM as your methodology, or otherwise using the SDM in a manner primarily intended for or directed toward commercial advantage or monetary compensation.

Suggested citation

Walsh, L. L. (2026). [Methodology preprint title]. Zenodo. https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20147000
