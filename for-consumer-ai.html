For Consumer AI & General Chatbots

You weren't built to be a mental health product. The law no longer cares.

General-purpose consumer AI products — companion chatbots, social platform AI, character-style products — are the segment most reshaped by the Garcia "product, not speech" ruling. You may not market yourself as a mental health tool. The conversations that produce wrongful-death claims and FTC inquiries don't care what your marketing says.

Who this page is for

This page is for companies whose product is general-purpose consumer AI — companion chatbots, character-style products, social-platform AI features, large consumer-facing conversational interfaces — where mental-health-adjacent conversation occurs incidentally, not by design. Trust & Safety, Product, Legal, and Risk leadership at these companies typically read this page first.

If your product has been named (or named-adjacent) in the FTC Sept 2025 6(b) inquiry, or in any state-AG investigation since, this page is for you.

What you face

Three changes since May 2025 that reset your category.

Garcia v. Character Technologies reclassified your product. In May 2025, Judge Anne C. Conway (M.D. Fla.) ruled that Character.AI is a product, not protected speech, for product-liability purposes. The First Amendment defense was rejected. Wrongful-death, product-liability, and Florida Deceptive and Unfair Trade Practices Act claims are all proceeding. This is the doctrinal hinge: consumer AI safety failures are now product-liability exposures, not content-moderation problems.

The FTC put your sector under inquiry. On Sept 11, 2025, the FTC issued 6(b) orders to seven companies — Alphabet, Character Technologies, Instagram, Meta, OpenAI, Snap, and xAI. The orders focus on safety assessments and risk mitigation for minors using AI companion chatbots. Section 5 deceptive-practices framework applies. Even companies not specifically named in that batch are reading the writing on the wall — this is a market-wide signal, not a one-off.

State-AG actions have escalated from investigation to litigation. Texas AG opened a deceptive-practices investigation in Aug 2025. Pennsylvania filed the first unlicensed-practice-of-medicine action in May 2026. The pattern is unmistakable: state AGs are testing the limits of consumer-protection and unlicensed-practice statutes against consumer AI companies whose products incidentally produce mental-health conversation.

The regulatory situation specific to you

Consumer AI is the test case for the entire post-Garcia regulatory framework.

  • Garcia v. Character Technologies (M.D. Fla., 6:24-cv-01903). "Product, not speech" ruling. First Amendment defense rejected. Wrongful-death claims proceeding. See full coverage on the regulation page →
  • FTC 6(b) inquiry (Sept 11, 2025). Active. Seven consumer AI companies under scrutiny. Section 5 deceptive-practices framework.
  • Pennsylvania v. Character Technologies (May 1, 2026). First state-AG action alleging unlicensed practice of medicine by an AI chatbot.
  • Texas AG investigation (Aug 18, 2025). Meta + Character.AI; deceptive trade practices around mental-health marketing.
  • CA SB 243 — Companion Chatbot Law (effective Jan 1, 2026). First comprehensive state law on companion chatbots. Disclosure, suicide/self-harm response protocols, crisis-resource referral, annual reporting. Largest US state market.
  • NY AI Companion Models Law (effective Nov 5, 2025). Up to $15,000/day in civil penalties. Crisis-detection and referral required.
  • UT HB 452. AI mental-health chatbot disclosure rules; can apply if your product's behavior satisfies the statutory definition of "mental health chatbot," even if you don't market it that way.
  • EU AI Act Article 5(1)(f). Emotion-recognition prohibition in force since Feb 2, 2025. Applies primarily to workplace and education contexts but reshapes the broader AI emotion-handling conversation.

Which engagement model fits

Consumer AI engagements usually combine red-team and ongoing review.

Failure-Mode & Red Team — Service 02 (see services page). The most common entry point for consumer AI. 4–8 weeks. Scenario-based stress testing against the SDM-defined high-risk states, with multi-version evaluation across your model releases. Where you are in your scaling curve

Consumer AI urgency moves with scale, not stage.

Pre-launch consumer AI is rare — most companies in this segment are already at scale before the safety questions get serious. If you're pre-launch with a consumer AI product that will plausibly encounter MH-adjacent conversation, a single Service 01 or Service 02 cycle pre-launch gives you the documented clinical evaluation that your insurer, your investors, and (eventually) your regulator will want to see.

Funded / scaling consumer AI is the largest segment in this category. Service 02 stress-test before a major user-base expansion or press cycle, plus an optional Service 03 retainer to keep evaluation evidence current as the product evolves.

Established consumer AI — large platforms, social companies, character-style products at scale — typically need continuous Service 03 plus Service 04 on standby. Engagement scope negotiated to match your release cadence, your user-base size, and your in-house Trust & Safety capacity.

What's at stake

The next high-profile case in this category will involve a company that "didn't think we were a mental health product."

That's the legal vulnerability specific to your segment. You can be a general-purpose AI product, you can have terms of service that explicitly disclaim therapeutic use, you can market yourself as entertainment or companionship — and your product can still produce the kind of conversation that ends up in a wrongful-death complaint or a state-AG press release. Garcia established that "we're not a mental health product" is not a defense. Pennsylvania v. Character.AI is testing the next layer: can an AI chatbot constitute the unlicensed practice of medicine even when the company never claimed to practice medicine?

Independent clinical evaluation, done before the incident, with citable methodology, is the evidence that holds up under that line of inquiry. It is not a guarantee. It is the defensible posture available — and the only one that gets cheaper, not more expensive, the earlier you build it.
