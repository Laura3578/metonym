Full deliverable

What a Clinical Risk Review engagement produces.

A typical Clinical Risk Review (Service 01) produces all of the following. Larger engagement models (Services 02–04) expand on this base.

  • Written report (typically 3–5 pages for a Risk Review; 8–20+ pages for Red Team or Expert Review engagements) — structured for both technical and non-technical readers, suitable for forwarding to safety officers, GCs, regulators, and boards.
  • Executive finding (1 page) — what we found, in plain language, with the headline finding and severity context.
  • MSS scoring table across the high-risk states evaluated, with severity scores and inadequate-response rates by state.
  • Representative findings with worked examples — actual transcript excerpts (under NDA) showing the clinical signal that was present, the system's response, and the clinical assessment of why it qualifies as inadequately recognized.
  • Prioritized recommendations — Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3 by clinical risk and remediation difficulty.
  • Methodology and limitations appendix — what we evaluated against, what we did not, and where the evaluation's known limitations lie. Cites the Zenodo preprint by DOI.
  • Regulatory mapping (optional) — explicit mapping of findings to relevant regulatory regimes (FDA CDS criteria, EU AI Act Annex III, state AI MH laws, MDR Annex XIV/XV). Especially useful for clients preparing submissions or anticipating audit.
  • Optional 60-minute clinical consult call with Dr. Walsh to walk through findings with your team, your retained counsel, or your safety committee.
  • Citable methodology reference — DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20147000 — that your team can include in internal documentation, regulatory submissions, and litigation work product.

How the deliverable gets used

Built to serve three readers at once.

  • The Safety Officer or Trust & Safety lead Uses the executive finding and prioritized recommendations to drive remediation. Uses the MSS scoring table to track changes across model versions over time.
  • The General Counsel or Risk officer Uses the report as evidence of independent clinical evaluation — defensible under litigation discovery, useful in regulatory inquiry, citable in internal governance.
  • The Product or Clinical lead Uses the worked findings and clinical assessment to translate evaluation results into engineering and product roadmap decisions.
  • (Where relevant) The Regulator or Auditor Uses the methodology appendix and regulatory mapping to verify that the evaluation meets independent-clinical-evaluation expectations under applicable frameworks.
