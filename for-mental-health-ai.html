For Mental-Health AI Products

You are in the most-regulated category of AI right now.

If your product is positioned as mental-health support — companionship, wellness, therapy-adjacent — the state, federal, and EU rules that came into force in the last 18 months apply directly to you. Documented clinical evaluation is moving from competitive advantage to table-stakes. Metonym is the firm built to produce that evidence.

Who this page is for

This page is for companies whose product is explicitly positioned as a mental-health, wellness, or emotional-support tool — companion chatbots, AI therapy-adjacent products, digital health companies adding AI mental-health features, and any startup whose marketing copy mentions stress, anxiety, mood, depression, support, or companionship in connection with AI conversation.

If your General Counsel has recently asked you to inventory which state AI laws apply to your product, this page is for you.

What you face

Three pressures specific to mental-health AI.

The regulatory wave is moving fastest in your direction. Illinois passed the strictest US AI-in-therapy restriction in Aug 2025. Utah passed AI mental-health chatbot disclosure rules effective May 2025. California's companion-chatbot law takes effect Jan 1, 2026. New York's AI Companion Models law has been in force since Nov 5, 2025 with daily penalties up to $15,000. The EU AI Act high-risk classification will apply to MH-positioned products from Aug 2026. No other AI category has this density of category-specific regulation.

State-AG enforcement has started. The Texas AG opened a public investigation in Aug 2025 of Meta and Character.AI specifically for "deceptive AI-generated mental health services." In May 2026 the Pennsylvania Department of State filed the first state action alleging unlicensed practice of medicine by an AI chatbot. If your product makes any claim that could read as therapeutic, you are in the enforcement zone — not approaching it, in it.

The clinical-evaluation question has become a buyer/regulator/board question, not an internal-quality question. Insurers ask for it. Enterprise procurement asks for it. Boards have started asking "have we been clinically evaluated?" The product can no longer answer "we have safety filters" and pass.

The regulatory situation specific to you

Every major US state-AI regime, plus the EU stack, applies to mental-health AI.

  • IL HB 1806 — Wellness & Oversight for Psychological Resources Act (signed Aug 4, 2025). Nation's strongest restriction on AI in therapy. Penalty up to $10,000 per violation, IDFPR enforcement.
  • UT HB 452 — AI Mental Health Chatbot Amendments (effective May 7, 2025). AI disclosure, ad disclosure, data-sharing restrictions. $2,500 per violation.
  • CA SB 243 — Companion Chatbot Law (effective Jan 1, 2026). Disclosure, suicide/self-harm response protocols, crisis-resource referral, annual reporting.
  • NY AI Companion Models Law (effective Nov 5, 2025). Up to $15,000/day in civil penalties to NY AG. Highest daily penalty in any current state regime.
  • TX TRAIGA (effective Jan 1, 2026). Texas comprehensive AI law; specific mental-health chatbot provisions ⚠️ verify against current statute.
  • FTC §5 inquiry (Sept 11, 2025). Active. Character Technologies and Meta among the seven companies under inquiry. Section 5 deceptive-practices framework explicitly invoked.
  • Pennsylvania v. Character Technologies (filed May 1, 2026). First US state-AG action alleging unlicensed practice of medicine by an AI chatbot. Watch closely; this case shapes the precedent for your category.
  • Garcia v. Character Technologies (M.D. Fla., May 2025). "Product, not speech" — First Amendment defense rejected. Product-liability theory survives.
  • EU AI Act Article 5(1)(f) + high-risk obligations. Emotion-recognition prohibition with medical/safety exception in force; high-risk obligations apply from Aug 2026 ⚠️ verify exact date. MDR may also apply if your product makes therapeutic claims.

Which engagement model fits

The right Metonym engagement depends on what you're trying to prove.

Pre-launch validation — Clinical Risk Review (Service 01). Fixed-scope, 2–4 week engagement. 25–50 scenarios. Written report citing the methodology preprint. The cleanest path to "documented clinical evaluation on file before launch."

Pre-launch + scale-up — Failure-Mode & Red Team (Service 02). 4–8 weeks. Adversarial-realistic scenario set across nine high-risk clinical states. Identifies the run-to-run variance that internal red teams average away. Right when your user base is about to grow significantly.

Ongoing safety integration — Safety Design & Co-Design Advisory (Service 03). 3–12 month retainer. Embedded clinical advisor. Right for products iterating frequently on response logic, escalation rules, or model versions.

Post-incident or pre-discovery — Expert Review (Service 04). Forensic-grade analysis under privilege coordination with your retained counsel. Right when something has already gone wrong, or when regulatory inquiry is imminent.

Where you are in your scaling curve

Stage shapes scope. The methodology doesn't change.

Pre-launch / pre-revenue startups. A fixed-scope Service 01 engagement is typically the right entry point — focused scenario set, written report you can show your board, regulator, and prospective insurer. Cost-calibrated to early-stage budgets. Crucially, having a dated clinical evaluation on file before launch is a meaningfully stronger defensive posture than producing one after a regulator letter arrives.

Funded / growth-stage companies. Service 02 makes sense as you scale toward larger user bases — your internal red team can keep pace with capability development, but the clinical evaluation work needs an external clinician who can identify the failure patterns your team doesn't have language for. Many companies at this stage combine an initial Service 02 with a Service 03 retainer afterward.

Established / public companies. Continuous Service 03 with periodic Service 02 stress-tests. Documentation suitable for SEC disclosure, board reporting, and regulatory submissions. Service 04 retained on standby for incident response.

What's at stake

Your category is the test case for every other AI sector.

Mental-health AI is where regulators, plaintiffs, and journalists are looking first. Decisions made now — in your company's specific evaluation posture, in your category's enforcement actions, in the case law your sector is generating — will set the template for how every other consumer-facing AI sector gets regulated downstream. You are not just managing your own risk. You are, by your evaluation posture, helping define what "reasonable diligence" means for the rest of the AI industry.

The companies that document independent clinical evaluation now will be the companies still operating when the dust settles. The cheaper version of this evaluation, prospectively, is the version of it you do before something happens.
