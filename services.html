01

Clinical Risk Review

Fixed-scope audit of how your system handles suicide risk in real conversations.

What you get

  • Review of 25–50 interactions or scenarios against the Salient Distress Model and Mechanical Severity Score framework
  • Identification of missed risk patterns and false reassurance patterns with worked examples
  • Performance-gap analysis benchmarked against established clinical frameworks
  • Written report (typically 3–5 pages) plus an optional 60-minute clinical consult call to walk through findings
  • Citable methodology reference (Zenodo DOI) for inclusion in your internal documentation

Engagement profile

  • Timeline: 2–4 weeks from signed engagement to delivered report
  • Scope: Fixed; expansion negotiated separately
  • Access required: Input/output surface only — no model weights or training data needed
  • Confidentiality: Written NDA before any system access
Best for Pre-deployment validation. Post-incident analysis. Ongoing safety assurance for products already in market. Boards or audit committees asking "have we been clinically evaluated?"

02

Failure-Mode & Red Team

Find where your system breaks under real psychological conditions — before a regulator, plaintiff, or journalist does.

What you get

  • Scenario-based stress testing across the high-risk and edge-case psychological states most likely to be misclassified
  • Identification of vulnerability and misclassification patterns specific to your system's response logic
  • Worked examples of high-risk misses, each with clinical explanation of the signal that was present and how it was missed
  • Targeted, prioritized recommendations for safety remediation
  • Optional re-test after remediation cycle

Engagement profile

  • Timeline: 4–8 weeks depending on scenario breadth and re-test cadence
  • Scope: Negotiated breadth (number of scenarios, number of model versions, number of personas)
  • Access required: Input/output surface; system-prompt visibility helpful but not required
  • Confidentiality: Standard engagement NDA; results not published or referenced externally without written consent
Best for Launch readiness. Scale-up safety review when a product is moving to a larger user base. Regulatory preparation. Pre-disclosure review before a press cycle. M&A diligence on a mental-health-adjacent AI product.

03

Safety Design & Co-Design Advisory

An embedded clinical advisor working with your product, safety, and engineering teams — clinical perspective at design time, not just at audit time.

What you get

  • Response-strategy and escalation-logic calibration grounded in clinical risk theory
  • Reduction of false positives (overcautious refusals) and false negatives (missed risk) at the decision-state level
  • System design for real-world transition risk: passive ideation → active planning, plan formation → method specification, etc.
  • Ongoing review of model behavior changes across releases and fine-tuning cycles
  • Documentation suitable for inclusion in regulatory submissions and internal safety governance

Engagement profile

  • Timeline: Ongoing retainer; minimum 3-month engagement, typically 6–12+ months
  • Scope: Defined deliverable cadence (e.g., monthly review sessions, quarterly written assessments)
  • Access required: Deeper system access negotiated in scoping with full IP protection
  • Confidentiality: Full embedded-advisor confidentiality, including coverage of design discussions and pre-release model behavior
Best for Teams building or recalibrating AI mental health features. Foundation model labs investing in safety infrastructure. Digital health companies whose product roadmap includes acute-risk-adjacent features.

04

Expert Review & Incident Analysis

Forensic-grade analysis when something has already gone wrong — or when discovery, inquiry, or inquiry-by-press is likely.

What you get

  • Transcript and system-behavior analysis conducted under appropriate confidentiality and (where applicable) privilege
  • Identification of missed or misinterpreted clinical signal, documented to forensic standards
  • Written opinion on foreseeability and failure points, structured for evidentiary use
  • Consultation with client counsel and, where retained, expert testimony
  • Coordination with internal and external investigators where appropriate

Engagement profile

  • Timeline: Calibrated to inquiry, litigation, or regulatory timeline; rush engagements available
  • Scope: Defined in coordination with retaining counsel
  • Access required: Full transcripts, system logs, and prior internal review materials
  • Confidentiality: Privilege-protected coordination with counsel; specialty work product
Best for Incident response. Regulatory inquiry. Pre-litigation strategy. Active litigation support. Internal investigation when the board has asked for clinical defensibility analysis.

How engagements run

A consistent process across all four engagement models.

  1. 30-minute scoping call. No charge, no proposal pressure. We get clear on what you're trying to evaluate, what compliance regime you're operating in, and what success looks like.
  2. Written scoping memo. Within 3 business days of the call, you receive a brief written scoping memo describing the recommended engagement model, scope, timeline, and pricing posture. Nothing is binding yet.
  3. Engagement letter and NDA. Once scope is agreed, we exchange a written engagement letter and (if not already in place) mutual NDA. Work begins on signing.
  4. Kickoff and access. Standing kickoff covers system access mechanics, scenario design, and internal point of contact. We set a check-in cadence appropriate to the engagement.
  5. Evaluation and review. The work itself, run against the SDM/MSS framework. Interim findings shared at agreed checkpoints; nothing is left for surprise at delivery.
  6. Delivery and consult. Written deliverable plus an optional clinical consult call to walk through findings with your team or with retained counsel.
  7. Optional re-test or follow-on. Many clients return after remediation cycles for re-evaluation or move from Service 01/02 into Service 03 ongoing advisory.
