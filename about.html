Dr. Laura L. Walsh is a licensed clinical psychologist with more than twenty years of practice, working across three domains where suicide risk shows up under high stakes: the consulting room, the courtroom, and now the AI safety frontier. Her forensic practice includes psychological evaluation in civil and employment matters — including wrongful death and suicide-related questions. That dual clinical-and-forensic background is what Metonym translates into AI evaluation.

The methodology now formalized as Metonym pairs the Salient Distress Model (SDM) — Walsh's clinical framework, openly published in the Zenodo methodology preprint — with the Mechanical Severity Score (MSS), a patent-pending scoring procedure. Together they emerged from systematic clinical evaluation of 1,759 multi-turn AI evaluation runs across 31+ active models on 53 scenarios (2,644 reference-annotated marker turns). The MSS scoring procedure and the broader evaluation pipeline are the subject of US Provisional Patent Application No. 64/059,837 (filed 2026-05-07); the empirical findings are published as a methodology preprint on Zenodo: DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20147000 (CC-BY-NC 4.0; preprint, not peer-reviewed — journal submission to follow).

Why this work

"My father was an engineer. From him I learned to approach complexity with patience and precision — slow down, look carefully at each layer, until something new emerges. That instinct runs through everything I do, whether I'm in session with a client, reviewing records for a deposition, or evaluating how an AI system misreads risk."

AI systems that interact with people in crisis are not a content-moderation problem. They are a clinical problem that happens to have been built by engineers. Metonym is the bridge — bringing clinical instruments to a domain that was previously evaluated only by engineering ones.

What Metonym is — and isn't

Metonym is a clinical evaluation firm. It is not a chatbot company, a content moderation platform, or a generic AI auditor. Its work is a clinical instrument applied to AI systems — and the deliverable a client receives is the kind of independent clinical assessment that regulators, ethics boards, and litigation discovery increasingly expect.

Dr. Walsh's clinical psychotherapy and forensic consulting practice — separate from Metonym's commercial AI evaluation work — operates under Walsh Psychology.

If your AI system might encounter someone in crisis, it should be clinically evaluated.
