Speaking · Training · Press

Speaking, training, and expert commentary on clinical AI safety.

Dr. Laura L. Walsh, PsyD speaks on the intersection of clinical psychology, AI safety, and the emerging regulatory landscape — for AI safety conferences, corporate trainings, legal CLEs, clinical CE programs, podcasts, and press interviews. Every engagement is led personally by Dr. Walsh.

Talk topics

Three talks, plus expert commentary on request.

01

What conventional AI safety misses — and what catches it

The clinical signals that filters were never trained on.

  • Why content moderation and clinical evaluation answer different questions
  • The decision-state transitions, post-decision calm, and temporal narrowing patterns that filters miss
  • What clinical instruments would catch instead
  • The 57% inadequate-response finding from 1,759 multi-turn evaluations across 31+ AI models
Best for AI safety conferences, foundation model lab internal training, Trust & Safety leadership offsites.

02

Clinical instruments for AI evaluation

Building reproducible safety metrics for systems that interact with people in distress.

  • The Salient Distress Model framework: what an evaluator should notice
  • The Mechanical Severity Score scoring procedure (patent-pending)
  • Translating clinical judgment into reproducible measurement across model versions
  • What rigorous AI evaluation evidence actually looks like
Best for AI/ML research groups, evaluation methodology conferences, clinical informatics audiences, academic seminars.

03

When an AI system encounters someone in crisis

Design, evaluation, and accountability for high-stakes AI interactions.

  • How clinical risk actually presents in real conversation — and why filters miss it
  • Design choices that improve recognition without producing brittle, over-cautious refusals
  • Evidence the engineering team, the GC, and the regulator each need
  • What accountability looks like when the system gets it wrong
Best for Cross-functional safety summits, digital health conferences, product leadership audiences, journalism roundtables.

04

Expert commentary & press interviews

For breaking news, regulatory developments, and high-profile AI safety incidents.

  • Clinical perspective on AI safety news cycles
  • Background briefings for reporters covering AI mental health
  • On-the-record commentary with appropriate attribution
  • Available for podcast guesting on AI safety, clinical psychology, and regulatory topics
Best for Print and broadcast journalism, AI safety podcasts, news analysis programs.

Engagement formats

From a 30-minute keynote to a half-day workshop.

  • Keynote — 30 to 60 minutes. Single talk, Q&A optional. Best for conference plenary sessions and corporate offsites.
  • Workshop — 90 minutes to a full day. Interactive, scenario-based, suitable for safety teams, engineering teams, and clinical informatics groups. Tailored to your system and your audience's prior knowledge.
  • Panel — Moderated panel participation. Standard panel fee structures apply.
  • Expert commentary — Press interviews (print, broadcast, podcast). On-the-record or background. Response window: typically 2 business days; rush available for breaking news.
  • CLE / CE-credit talks — Continuing legal education for in-house counsel and litigators; continuing education for licensed mental-health and healthcare professionals. CE accreditation handled by host organization.
  • Internal training — Custom training for foundation model labs, digital health companies, and AI safety teams. Confidentiality terms negotiated; content adapted to your system and stack.
